Day 1: Full-Body Strength Training

Warm-up: 5-10 minutes of dynamic stretching or light cardio (e.g., jogging in place, jumping jacks).
Squats: 3 sets of 10-12 repetitions
Push-ups or chest presses (using dumbbells or resistance bands): 3 sets of 10-12 repetitions
Bent-over rows (using dumbbells or resistance bands): 3 sets of 10-12 repetitions
Lunges (alternating legs): 3 sets of 10-12 repetitions per leg
Plank: Hold for 30-60 seconds
Cool-down: 5-10 minutes of static stretching focusing on major muscle groups.

Day 2: Cardiovascular Exercise

Choose a cardiovascular activity such as running, cycling, swimming, or rowing.
Perform 30-60 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio.
Focus on maintaining a steady pace and challenging yourself based on fitness level.
Cool-down: 5-10 minutes of light cardio followed by stretching.

Day 3: Active Recovery or Flexibility

Engage in low-impact activities such as yoga, Pilates, or gentle stretching.
Focus on improving flexibility, mobility, and relaxation.
Incorporate foam rolling or self-myofascial release techniques to alleviate muscle tension and soreness.

Day 4: Upper Body Strength Training

Warm-up: 5-10 minutes of dynamic stretching or light cardio.
Bench press or chest flyes (using dumbbells or resistance bands): 3 sets of 10-12 repetitions
Pull-ups or lat pulldowns (using resistance bands or machine): 3 sets of 8-10 repetitions
Shoulder press (using dumbbells or resistance bands): 3 sets of 10-12 repetitions
Bicep curls (using dumbbells or resistance bands): 3 sets of 10-12 repetitions
Tricep dips or tricep extensions (using dumbbells or resistance bands): 3 sets of 10-12 repetitions
Cool-down: 5-10 minutes of static stretching focusing on upper body muscles.

Day 5: Cardiovascular Exercise

Choose a different cardiovascular activity than Day 2 or vary the intensity.
Perform 30-60 minutes of cardio focusing on interval training or high-intensity intervals (HIIT) for added intensity.
Cool-down: 5-10 minutes of light cardio followed by stretching.

Day 6: Lower Body Strength Training

Warm-up: 5-10 minutes of dynamic stretching or light cardio.
Deadlifts (using dumbbells or barbell): 3 sets of 8-10 repetitions
Leg press or goblet squats (using dumbbells or resistance bands): 3 sets of 10-12 repetitions
Romanian deadlifts (using dumbbells or barbell): 3 sets of 10-12 repetitions
Calf raises: 3 sets of 12-15 repetitions
Leg curls (using resistance bands or machine): 3 sets of 10-12 repetitions
Cool-down: 5-10 minutes of static stretching focusing on lower body muscles.

Day 7: Rest or Active Recovery

Take a day off from structured exercise to allow the body to rest and recover.
Engage in light activities such as walking, leisurely biking, or gentle stretching if desired.
Notes:

Progressively increase the intensity, duration, or weight lifted as you become stronger and more comfortable with the exercises.
Listen to your body and modify exercises as needed to accommodate any injuries, limitations, or discomfort.
Stay hydrated and fuel your body with nutritious foods to support energy levels and recovery.
Consult with a fitness professional or healthcare provider before starting any new exercise program, especially if you have pre-existing medical conditions or concerns.
