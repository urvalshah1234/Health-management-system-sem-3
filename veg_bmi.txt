Breakfast:

Overnight oats made with rolled oats, chia seeds, almond milk, sliced bananas, and a sprinkle of cinnamon.
A glass of freshly squeezed orange juice or a piece of whole fruit.
Mid-Morning Snack:

Greek yogurt or coconut yogurt topped with mixed berries (such as strawberries, blueberries, or raspberries) and a handful of granola or nuts.
Lunch:

Quinoa salad with mixed vegetables (such as bell peppers, cucumbers, cherry tomatoes, and avocado) dressed with a lemon-tahini dressing.
A serving of baked tofu or chickpeas for added protein.
Afternoon Snack:

Sliced apple or carrot sticks with hummus or almond butter.
A small handful of almonds or cashews for additional energy.
Dinner:

Stir-fried vegetables (such as broccoli, bell peppers, mushrooms, and snap peas) with tofu or tempeh in a light soy-ginger sauce, served over brown rice or quinoa.
Steamed edamame or a side salad with mixed greens and balsamic vinaigrette dressing.
Evening Snack (Optional):

A smoothie made with spinach, banana, frozen berries, almond milk, and a scoop of plant-based protein powder.
Whole grain crackers with avocado slices and cherry tomatoes.
Hydration:

Drink plenty of water throughout the day to stay hydrated.
Include herbal teas or infused water for variety.
Notes:

Focus on whole, nutrient-dense foods such as fruits, vegetables, whole grains, legumes, nuts, and seeds.
Aim to incorporate a variety of colors, textures, and flavors into your meals for optimal nutrition.
Monitor portion sizes and listen to your body's hunger and fullness cues.
Consider consulting with a registered dietitian or nutritionist for personalized dietary advice and guidance on maintaining a healthy body.
